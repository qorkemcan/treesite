# Florida Tree Identification for Homeowners: Common Yard Trees and What They Can Tell You

A tree name is useful only when the identification is reliable enough for the decision being made.

A probable identification can guide research into mature size, roots, pests, pruning, fruit, invasive status, and site fit. It does not prove that the tree is safe, diseased, protected, invasive, or removable.

Use a layered process: location, overall plant type, leaf arrangement, leaf structure, bark, reproductive features, habitat, and several clear photographs.

## Begin with the broadest split

| First observation | Likely group | Useful next clue |
|---|---|---|
| One main trunk with fronds emerging from a crown | Palm | Fan or feather fronds, trunk, crownshaft, fruit. |
| Needle-like leaves in bundles with woody cones | True pine | Needles per bundle, cone, bark, region. |
| Scale-like or awl-like foliage with cones | Cypress, juniper, or another conifer | Cone type, twig arrangement, habitat. |
| Broad leaves on woody branches | Broadleaf tree | Opposite or alternate leaves; simple or compound. |
| Jointed green branchlets that resemble needles | May not be a true pine | Fruit, branchlets, bark, invasive-status sources. |
| Multi-stem woody plant | Tree or large shrub | Mature form, leaves, flowers, fruit. |

Do not call every tall plant with narrow leaves a pine or every single-trunk tropical plant a palm.

## What to photograph

For a useful identification set, take photos of:

- the whole tree from a distance,
- bark and trunk,
- leaf arrangement on the twig,
- both sides of leaves,
- flowers, fruit, seed pods, or cones,
- the base and root flare,
- nearby seedlings or suckers,
- the surrounding site.

For invasive lookalike examples, see the Brazilian pepper, camphor, carrotwood, Chinese tallow, melaleuca, Australian pine, and earleaf acacia guides.

## Identification is not the final decision

| Question | Identification helps with | What it does not prove |
|---|---|---|
| Is this tree invasive? | It points to the right status source. | Whether removal is authorized on this site. |
| Is this tree safe? | It explains common traits. | Current structural condition. |
| Can I remove it? | It helps with permit research. | Local approval or legal authority. |
| Should I prune it? | It guides species-sensitive care. | Whether a limb is safe to cut. |
