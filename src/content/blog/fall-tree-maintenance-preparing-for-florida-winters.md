# Fall Tree Maintenance: Preparing for Florida Winters

Florida winters are not harsh in the way many other states experience them, but that does not mean fall tree care is optional.

In Florida, fall is one of the most useful maintenance windows homeowners get all year. The most intense summer growth has usually slowed, the yard has already been through heat, rain, and storm pressure, and the property is in a better position for a calm, practical review.

Small problems stand out more clearly. Old storm damage that never got handled properly becomes easier to notice. Trees that looked “probably fine” in summer may now show subtle stress or structural imbalance before winter and before the next active season.

## Why fall matters for Florida tree care

Fall maintenance is less about snow-climate winter prep and more about post-summer review.

Use the season to:

- inspect after heat and storm pressure,
- clean up unresolved storm damage,
- reduce deadwood and nuisance limbs,
- correct mulch and root-flare problems,
- notice declining trees before the next growing season,
- make practical decisions while weather is calmer.

For storm-related inspection, see [What a Tree Inspection Should Include Before Hurricane Season](/blog/what-a-tree-inspection-should-include-before-hurricane-season/) and [Pruning Before Storm Season](/blog/pruning-before-storm-season-what-florida-homeowners-should-know/).

## Fall inspection checklist

| Area | What to look for |
|---|---|
| Canopy | Deadwood, broken branches, hanging limbs, uneven weight. |
| Trunk | Cracks, cavities, fungal growth, wounds, fresh movement. |
| Base | Root flare buried by mulch, soil changes, mushrooms, heaving. |
| Soil | Saturation, erosion, washout, compaction. |
| Clearance | Roof, gutters, fence, driveway, sidewalk, and access routes. |
| Stumps | Old tripping hazards or areas needing grinding before replanting. |
