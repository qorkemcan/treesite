Storm-damaged trees can shift without warning. The safest first move is to keep people away from the area and avoid any DIY cutting.

## Step 1: Create distance

Keep children, pets, and vehicles away from the damaged tree. If large limbs are hanging overhead, treat the area as unstable.

## Step 2: Watch for line hazards

If branches are touching a power line or a service drop, do not go near them. Contact the utility provider first if there is any chance the line is involved.

## Step 3: Document the scene

Take photos before cleanup begins. This helps with both insurance documentation and contractor communication.

## Step 4: Get a professional assessment

A leaning trunk, split union, or root plate movement can mean the tree is no longer structurally safe.

> In Florida, storm response decisions often need to be made quickly, but safety comes before cleanup speed.

## Final takeaway

The first goal is not appearance. It is reducing immediate risk and preventing a second failure.
