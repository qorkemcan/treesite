# What to Do If a Tree Blocks Your Driveway After a Storm

Do not begin by cutting a path for the car.

First check for power lines, suspended limbs, a partly attached trunk, lifted roots, movement at the base, and any part of the tree resting on another tree or structure. If a line is down or a tree is touching electrical equipment, stay away and call 911 and the utility as appropriate.

A tree that is fully down and stable may be a priority cleanup job. A tree that is only partly down can be an active failure under stored tension. Both block the driveway, but they are not the same hazard.

## The first five minutes

### Keep everyone back

Move people, pets, and vehicles away from the tree. Do not allow someone to climb over the trunk or walk beneath broken limbs simply because the driveway is the normal exit.

If the driveway is the only safe way out and a medical or fire emergency exists, call 911. Tell the dispatcher that storm debris or a tree is blocking access.

### Look for electrical involvement

From a safe distance, check for:

- downed wires,
- branches touching wires,
- damaged service drop to the house,
- broken poles or transformers,
- sparking, smoke, buzzing, or arcing,
- a tree resting in a utility corridor.

Never assume a line is de-energized. Read [what to do if a tree is touching power lines after a storm](/blog/what-to-do-if-a-tree-is-touching-power-lines-after-a-storm/) before approaching any line-adjacent tree.

## Decide whether this is emergency or scheduled work

[Emergency response services](/services/emergency-response/) may be appropriate when:

| Situation | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Driveway is the only access | Residents, emergency vehicles, or repairs may be blocked. |
| Tree is partly suspended | It may shift while being cut or pulled. |
| Limb hangs over driveway | People may walk under it without thinking. |
| Root plate is lifting | The tree may still be failing. |
| Tree rests on roof or vehicle | More damage may occur if it shifts. |
| Utilities are involved | Utility-first boundary applies. |

If the tree is fully down, stable, and the area can be avoided, ordinary [tree removal services](/services/tree-removal/) may be enough.

## Do not pull it with a vehicle

A truck, SUV, chain, or rope can make the situation worse. Storm-damaged trees may be under hidden tension. Pulling can move the tree into a vehicle, fence, garage, or person.

For suspended wood, see [what is a hung-up tree?](/blog/what-is-a-hung-up-tree-and-why-storm-damaged-trees-are-dangerous-to-touch/).

## Take safe photos

If it is safe, photograph:

- the whole tree,
- the driveway blockage,
- utility proximity,
- root movement,
- broken limbs overhead,
- roof or vehicle contact,
- before-cleanup debris position,
- completed emergency work.

For insurance timing, see [should you call insurance or tree service first?](/blog/should-you-call-insurance-or-a-tree-service-first-after-storm-damage/).
