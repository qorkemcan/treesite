# Large Tree Removal Near Power Lines: Call the Utility Before a Tree Crew

Assume every power line is energized.

If a tree, branch, rope, tool, vehicle, fence, or standing water may contact electrical equipment, do not approach or begin tree work.

The electric utility controls the electrical hazard. A private tree-service provider should enter only after the utility identifies the safe scope and any required line-clearance qualifications, de-energization, grounding, or service-drop work.

## Use the utility-first hierarchy

| Condition | First action | Private tree-service role |
|---|---|---|
| Downed line | Call 911 and electric utility; stay far away | None until utility releases the scene |
| Tree or branch touching a line | Contact electric utility; 911 for immediate life danger | None while contact may be energized |
| Arcing, fire, smoke, or pulled meter/service mast | Evacuate and call 911/utility | After fire and electrical control |
| Tree within possible movement range of distribution lines | Ask utility for vegetation and safety instructions | Qualified scope after utility review |
| Branch near service drop | Ask utility who owns and controls the line and whether temporary disconnection is needed | Work only under utility instructions |
| Communication cable only | Confirm line identity with providers; do not guess | Controlled tree work after electrical hazards are excluded |
| Tree outside corridor but could fall into lines | Utility consultation and qualified removal plan | Rigging/removal that cannot enter the electrical hazard |
| Underground facilities near stump or equipment | Sunshine 811 and private locate as needed | Grind, excavate, or place equipment only after locate process |

Do not use visual distance as the final decision.

## Stay away from downed lines and energized ground

A downed conductor can energize:

- soil
- water
- tree
- fence
- vehicle
- metal structure
- debris
- nearby equipment

Do not:

- touch the line
- touch the tree
- move the branch
- drive over the line
- step into water
- retrieve a pet
- approach a vehicle in contact
- use a wooden tool or rope
- assume the line is insulated or dead

Call 911 and the electric utility.

Follow dispatcher instructions.

## Distribution line, service drop, and communications cable

Homeowners should not identify lines by appearance.

Ask the utility:

- Is this an electric distribution conductor?
- Is it a service drop?
- Is it a communication cable?
- Who owns the pole?
- Who owns the service connection?
- Is the line energized?
- Will the utility trim?
- Is temporary disconnection available?
- Is a line-clearance arborist required?
- What clearance and work sequence are required?
- Who reconnects service?
- What tree debris remains the owner’s responsibility?

The line to the house is not automatically a low-risk line.

## Only qualified line-clearance professionals should work in the electrical zone

FPL states that only specially trained line-clearing professionals should work around power lines.

OSHA defines and regulates line-clearance tree-trimming operations near energized electric supply lines and equipment. Qualified workers must follow minimum approach distances and other electrical-safety requirements.

A general tree-removal crew, landscaper, handyman, or homeowner should not improvise inside that hazard.

Ask the utility and provider to identify:

- line-clearance qualification
- employer safety program
- utility authorization
- minimum approach distance
- job briefing
- aerial-lift controls
- rescue plan
- de-energization
- grounding
- observer requirements
- weather stop conditions

Do not accept “we do this all the time” as the safety plan.

## Ten feet is not a homeowner DIY permission line

OSHA and utility materials often use distance thresholds for trained workers and regulated operations.

A homeowner should not measure ten feet and decide ordinary work is safe.

Tree sections can move because of:

- wind
- branch swing
- trunk rotation
- hinge failure
- rigging stretch
- crane movement
- root movement
- support release
- equipment position
- incorrect height estimate

Call the utility whenever the tree or any part of the work could enter the line area.

## Large trees need a movement plan

The removal plan should show:

- tree height
- canopy radius
- lean
- line location
- voltage and facility information from the utility
- service drop
- pole and transformer
- structures
- road
- property line
- crane
- rigging
- landing zone
- exclusion zone
- equipment setup
- escape and rescue access

A branch that is clear before cutting may swing into the line after separation.

## Storm-damaged trees near lines

Storm damage may create:

- hanging branch
- lodged tree
- split trunk
- root-plate lift
- conductor contact
- broken pole
- pulled service equipment
- wet-ground exposure
- support-tree dependence

Do not attempt storm cleanup before the electrical condition is controlled.

Use [How to Make a Florida Tree Emergency Safer Before Help Arrives](/blog/how-to-make-a-florida-tree-emergency-safer-before-help-arrives/).

## De-energization and line-drop coordination

The utility may determine that work requires:

- scheduled outage
- temporary service disconnection
- line guard or cover under utility procedure
- utility crew onsite
- utility vegetation contractor
- pole or equipment repair
- conductor relocation
- service-mast repair
- customer electrician
- reconnection inspection

The tree provider should not promise electrical control on the utility’s behalf.

Confirm:

- request number
- date
- outage window
- responsible parties
- prerequisites
- reconnection process
- cancellation conditions
- tree-work limits

## Underground facilities

Large removals may involve:

- stump grinding
- root excavation
- crane outriggers
- mats
- grading
- replacement planting
- fence removal
- access widening

Contact Sunshine 811 at least two full business days before covered excavation. The request day, weekends, and holidays are handled under the applicable process.

Sunshine 811 notifies member operators. Private irrigation, lighting, pool, septic, gate, and customer-owned lines may require a private locate.

Keep the ticket and positive responses.

## Weather can stop the job

Tree and line work should pause when conditions create unacceptable risk, including:

- lightning
- high wind
- heavy rain
- saturated setup area
- poor visibility
- tropical-storm conditions
- unstable tree movement
- flooding
- damaged utility equipment
- loss of safe access

Emergency urgency does not make unsafe electrical work acceptable.

## Estimate and contract checklist

The written scope should identify:

- utility contact
- facility type
- line-clearance role
- de-energization or service drop
- electrician if needed
- tree condition
- crane and rigging
- access
- underground locate
- private locate
- property protection
- traffic control
- exclusion zone
- stump work
- debris
- restoration
- permits
- cancellation
- utility delay
- emergency escalation

Do not schedule the tree crew and hope the utility coordination happens later.

## If a tree is burning or arcing

Evacuate.

Call 911 and the utility.

Do not use:

- garden hose
- extinguisher near electrical contact
- chainsaw
- vehicle
- rope
- ladder
- metal tool

Do not approach until responders say the area is safe.
