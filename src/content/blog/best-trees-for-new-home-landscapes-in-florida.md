# Best Trees for New Home Landscapes in Florida

A new home landscape gives homeowners something rare: **the chance to get the tree decisions right from the beginning.**

That matters more than people think.

Most long-term tree problems do not start with bad intentions. They start with small nursery trees planted too close to the house, utilities, driveways, sidewalks, septic areas, pool plans, or future access paths. The tree looked harmless on planting day. Years later, the site fit became the real problem.

That is why the best trees for a new Florida home landscape are not just the prettiest or fastest-growing trees at the garden center. They are the trees that still make sense when the yard is five, ten, and twenty years older.

## The first rule: right tree, right place

A new lot feels open, bare, and full of possibility. That often tempts homeowners to plant quickly and plant big.

Slow down and choose trees based on:

- mature height and canopy spread,
- available root space,
- sun exposure,
- drainage,
- distance from the house,
- distance from driveways and walks,
- overhead and underground utility conflicts,
- septic or drainage areas,
- future pool, patio, or addition plans,
- how the yard will actually be used.

For related planning, see [Best Trees to Plant Under Power Lines in Florida](/blog/best-trees-to-plant-under-power-lines-in-florida/) and [Best Trees for Septic Areas and Drain Fields in Florida](/blog/best-trees-for-septic-areas-and-drain-fields-in-florida/).

## New-home decision table

| Choice | Why it matters later |
|---|---|
| Planting too close to the house | Creates future roof, gutter, root, and access conflicts. |
| Choosing only for fast growth | Fast can become oversized or brittle. |
| Ignoring drainage | Wet and dry cycles affect survival and stability. |
| Forgetting utilities | Digging and mature roots can conflict with lines. |
| Planting before hardscape plans | Future pavers, pools, and patios may crowd roots. |
| Skipping mature-size research | Nursery size is not the final tree. |
