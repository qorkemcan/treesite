# Can You Throw Neighbor Tree Branches Back Over the Fence in Florida?

The lower-conflict answer is **no—do not throw cut branches, logs, palm material, or yard debris onto a neighbor’s property without an agreement**.

The branch may have grown from the neighbor’s tree, but trimming authority, ownership of cut material, disposal rules, and permission to place debris on another parcel are separate questions.

A safer plan is:

1. confirm the property line and pruning scope,
2. communicate before cutting,
3. ask whether the neighbor wants the wood,
4. use local yard-waste rules or contractor haul-away,
5. document large, storm-related, or disputed work.

This is practical homeowner guidance, not legal advice. If the dispute involves property damage, unauthorized cutting, boundary uncertainty, or a shared tree, get local legal guidance.

## Choose the disposal path before trimming

| Material or situation | Practical disposal path | Main caution |
|---|---|---|
| Small hand-pruned twigs | Local yard-waste container or bundle rules | Size and collection rules vary. |
| Large branch or log | Contractor haul-away or agreed wood transfer | Heavy material can damage fences, irrigation, or property. |
| Palm fronds | Local yard-waste process or haul-away | Fronds may have separate preparation rules. |
| Storm debris | Follow current city or county storm instructions | Normal collection rules may be suspended. |
| Neighbor wants the wood | Agree on placement and transfer method in writing | Do not toss material over a fence. |
| Dangerous or loaded limb | Professional controlled removal | Do not treat it as ordinary yard debris. |

## When the branch is not ordinary yard debris

If a branch is cracked over a roof, loaded after a storm, tangled in a fence, near power lines, or too heavy to control safely, [emergency response services](/services/emergency-response/) may be appropriate.

If the larger tree is dead, unstable, or repeatedly causing dangerous overhang, [tree removal services](/services/tree-removal/) may become part of the conversation. For shared properties, rentals, HOAs, and commercial sites, [commercial tree services](/services/commercial-services/) may help document access, authority, and disposal expectations.
