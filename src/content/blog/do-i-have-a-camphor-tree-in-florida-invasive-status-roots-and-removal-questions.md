# Do I Have a Camphor Tree in Florida? Invasive Status, Roots, and Removal Questions

Camphor tree has different current UF/IFAS recommendations by region.

- **North and Central Florida:** Invasive and not recommended.
- **South Florida:** Caution—manage to prevent escape; reassessment is in process.

That regional status matters, but it does not prove that every existing camphor tree is unsafe or must be removed. The homeowner decision should combine identity, Florida region, volunteer spread, tree condition, location, hardscape conflicts, local authority, and the long-term landscape plan.

## Identify the tree without relying on odor alone

Camphor tree, *Cinnamomum camphora*, is an evergreen tree in the laurel family.

| Feature | Useful camphor clue |
|---|---|
| Leaves | Alternate, glossy, oval-to-elliptic leaves. |
| Veins | Three prominent veins often arise near the leaf base. |
| New growth | May emerge reddish or bronze. |
| Fruit | Small dark drupes on branching stalks. |
| Bark | Gray-brown and increasingly furrowed with age. |
| Odor | Crushed tissue may smell strongly of camphor. |

Odor can support identification, but do not make crushed leaves the only test. Photograph the complete leaf, veins, twig arrangement, fruit, bark, and whole tree. Compare with other evergreen shade trees through [Florida Tree Identification for Homeowners](/blog/florida-tree-identification-for-homeowners-common-yard-trees-and-what-they-can-tell-you/).

## When removal becomes a serious option

| Issue | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Volunteer seedlings spreading | Regional invasive status and management matter. |
| Roots lifting pavement | Hardscape conflict may worsen. |
| Tree too close to the house | Clearance and root space may be poor. |
| Decline, decay, or lean | Tree-risk assessment may be needed. |
| Replacement plan exists | Removal can be paired with better site-fit planting. |

[Tree trimming services](/services/tree-trimming/) may help with clearance and deadwood, but pruning does not solve invasive spread or poor placement. [Tree removal services](/services/tree-removal/) may be appropriate when the tree is poorly located, declining, spreading, or causing significant site conflict.
