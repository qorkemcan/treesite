# Boundary Trees: Can You Trim Your Neighbor’s Overhanging Limbs?

Florida neighbor-tree disputes are often discussed through a self-help rule: an adjoining owner may generally address branches or roots that encroach onto that owner’s property, at that owner’s expense.

That shorthand is not a complete work order.

Before pruning, confirm the boundary, stay off the neighbor’s property without permission, avoid cutting beyond the line, and determine whether the proposed cuts could seriously damage or destabilize the tree. A disputed boundary, a trunk on the line, protected vegetation, utility lines, HOA authority, or major structural limbs can change the path.

This is practical homeowner guidance, not legal advice. For a disputed boundary, injury claim, shared trunk, or high-value tree, speak with a qualified local professional or attorney.

## Use this decision table

| Condition | Safer first step | Do not do |
|---|---|---|
| Small live branch crosses a known line | Plan a limited cut using proper pruning practices | Cross the line or leave damaging stubs |
| Large limb extends over roof or driveway | Obtain a qualified written pruning scope | Remove the entire scaffold limb without assessing tree response |
| Branch is dead, cracked, or storm-loaded | Restrict the target area and document it | Stand beneath it or attempt uncontrolled DIY cutting |
| Property line is uncertain | Review survey evidence | Treat the fence as automatic proof |
| Trunk appears to straddle the line | Pause and obtain survey/legal advice | Assume one owner may remove the tree alone |
| Limb is near power lines | Contact the utility | Hire an ordinary crew to work inside a line-clearance hazard |
| HOA/common-area tree is involved | Confirm authority and approval | Treat it as a two-neighbor-only issue |
| Mangrove or regulated coastal vegetation | Contact DEP or delegated authority | Apply ordinary overhang rules |

## Trimming scope matters

[Tree trimming services](/services/tree-trimming/) may be appropriate when the cut is limited, safe, and does not cross the property line or destabilize the tree.

But trimming can become a bigger problem when it removes too much live canopy, creates structural imbalance, harms a protected tree, or effectively destroys the neighbor’s tree.

For broader trimming rules, see [Do You Need a Permit to Trim a Tree in Florida?](/blog/do-you-need-a-permit-to-trim-a-tree-in-florida/) and [The Right Way to Prune Trees Before Storm Season](/blog/the-right-way-to-prune-trees-before-storm-season/).

## Emergency and removal situations

If a branch is cracked over a roof, hanging after a storm, or blocking access, [emergency response services](/services/emergency-response/) may be needed. Document safely before cleanup.

[Tree removal services](/services/tree-removal/) should not be treated as a neighbor-only decision when the trunk, ownership, authority, or legal boundary is unclear.
