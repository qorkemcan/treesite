# Questions to Ask Before Hiring a Tree Removal Service in Florida

A useful hiring conversation should tell you three things: who is responsible, how the tree will be handled, and what the property will look like when the crew leaves.

You do not need to interview a company for an hour. These questions cover the decisions most likely to affect safety, price, cleanup, and disputes.

The goal is not to make the conversation adversarial. The goal is to avoid surprises before [tree removal services](/services/tree-removal/) begin.

## 1. Can you provide current insurance and workers’ compensation information?

Ask for current general liability documentation and workers’ compensation coverage or applicable exemption information. Confirm that the business name on the documents matches the estimate.

Useful follow-up questions include:

- Does the policy cover the type of tree work being proposed?
- Are subcontractors covered?
- Will a crane or separate equipment provider be involved?
- Can the insurance agent or carrier confirm the certificate?

Insurance does not prove skill, but vague or inconsistent documents are a reason to pause. For a deeper verification process, read [Licensed and Insured Tree Service in Florida](/blog/licensed-and-insured-tree-service-in-florida-what-homeowners-should-check/).

## 2. Who will actually perform and supervise the work?

The estimator, crew, climber, stump-grinding operator, and crane company may be different people or businesses.

Ask:

- Who is the on-site supervisor?
- Are workers employees or subcontractors?
- Who decides if the method changes?
- Who is responsible for communicating with the homeowner?

This matters most on complex jobs near homes, driveways, pool cages, fences, power lines, or neighboring property.

## 3. How will the tree be removed?

A clear estimate should explain the method, not just the price.

Ask whether the job involves:

| Method question | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Climbing | Equipment may not reach the tree. |
| Bucket truck | Setup and driveway access matter. |
| Crane assistance | Scheduling and load planning can affect price. |
| Rigging | Limbs may need controlled lowering. |
| Hand carrying | Tight access can increase labor. |
| Ground protection | Pavers, sod, and irrigation may need protection. |

For method context, see [bucket truck, climber, or crane](/blog/bucket-truck-climber-or-crane-why-tree-removal-methods-change-the-quote/) and [what is a crane pick?](/blog/what-is-a-crane-pick-in-tree-removal-and-why-does-it-affect-the-quote/).

## 4. What will the property look like when the crew leaves?

Ask exactly what happens to:

- brush,
- palm fronds,
- logs,
- trunk pieces,
- chips,
- stump grindings,
- ruts,
- pavers,
- irrigation,
- fence panels,
- final raking and blowing.

For cleanup details, see [tree removal cleanup quote scope](/blog/tree-removal-cleanup-what-should-be-included-in-the-quote/) and [wood and large logs after tree removal](/blog/what-happens-to-the-wood-and-large-logs-after-tree-removal-your-options-explained/).

## 5. Is stump grinding included?

[Stump grinding services](/services/stump-grinding/) are often separate from tree removal.

Ask:

- Is stump grinding included?
- How deep will the stump be ground?
- Are surface roots included?
- Can the grinder reach the stump?
- Are chips hauled, left, or backfilled?
- Is replanting planned?

If the stump is near pavers, irrigation, septic, fences, or a pool deck, access and private-line issues matter.

## 6. Are permits, HOA rules, or local requirements involved?

The tree service can explain what they commonly see, but the homeowner should verify current local requirements directly when permits, HOA rules, easements, protected trees, or right-of-way questions may apply.

For Florida permit context, see [Florida tree removal permit guide](/blog/florida-tree-removal-permit-guide/).

## 7. What happens if the job becomes more complicated?

Ask how change orders are handled if crews find decay, hidden metal, utility conflicts, storm tension, unstable wood, or access problems.

For quote-risk context, see [why the cheapest tree removal quote can become the most expensive](/blog/why-the-cheapest-tree-removal-quote-can-become-the-most-expensive/) and [low tree removal quote checklist](/blog/low-tree-removal-quote-checklist-what-florida-homeowners-should-confirm-before-saying-yes/).

## 8. What if this is urgent or storm-related?

If a tree is on a structure, blocking access, hanging overhead, touching wires, or actively moving, [emergency response services](/services/emergency-response/) may be needed before ordinary scheduling.

For rentals, associations, business properties, or multi-site work, [commercial tree services](/services/commercial-services/) may help with notices, access, staging, and documentation.

If multiple trees or heavy brush are involved, [land clearing services](/services/land-clearing/) may be more appropriate than a single-tree request.
