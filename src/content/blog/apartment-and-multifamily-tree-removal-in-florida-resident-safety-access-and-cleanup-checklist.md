# Apartment and Multifamily Tree Removal in Florida: Resident Safety, Access, and Cleanup Checklist

Multifamily tree work is an **occupied-site operation**.

A good plan must protect:

- residents
- visitors
- children and pets
- parked vehicles
- entrances
- sidewalks
- fire lanes
- mail and delivery access
- pools and amenities
- maintenance staff
- the final landscape

The distinct question is not only how to cut the tree. It is how to control a work zone inside a community where people continue to live.

## Use this multifamily operations table

| Resident or property issue | What the plan should define |
|---|---|
| Parking beneath or near the tree | Spaces to clear, move deadline, towing policy contact |
| Building entrance | Closure and alternate accessible route |
| Sidewalk or breezeway | Barriers, detour, and reopening |
| Pool, playground, or dog park | Full closure boundary and inspection before reopening |
| Fire lane | Continuous availability or approved coordination |
| School-bus or pickup area | Work scheduled outside use period |
| Gated property | Equipment dimensions, gate access, resident traffic |
| Balcony or patio | Resident notice and item-removal instructions |
| Emergency tree failure | Immediate exclusion and stabilization |
| Debris and stump | Safe staging, hauling, barricade, restoration |

## Separate routine, planned, and emergency work

### Routine

- deadwood removal
- roof clearance
- palm maintenance
- sidewalk clearance
- limited structural pruning

### Planned removal

- stable dead tree
- chronic hardscape conflict
- documented structural decline
- replacement project
- tree no longer practical to retain

### Emergency stabilization

- tree on a building
- blocked primary entrance
- hanging major limb
- active split
- tree loading a carport
- electrical contact
- root-plate movement

Emergency stabilization can occur before the complete removal, hauling, and stump phases.

## Secure the area

For an active hazard:

- close the affected walkway
- clear nearby parking
- keep residents away from suspended wood
- close the pool, playground, dog area, or courtyard when exposed
- maintain fire and emergency access
- contact 911 and the utility where appropriate
- use physical barriers appropriate to the hazard
- assign a property contact

A strip of caution tape is not a complete control plan when residents can walk around it into the drop zone.

## Resident communication

A planned notice should include:

- date and work window
- affected buildings
- parking spaces to clear
- entrances or sidewalks closed
- amenity closures
- pet and child restrictions
- noise expectations
- equipment route
- delivery or mail changes
- contact person
- weather-delay plan
- expected reopening

For emergency work, notify residents as soon as practical after the immediate area is controlled.

Avoid legal conclusions in the work notice. State the operational facts.

## Dwelling-unit access

Most exterior tree work should not require broad entry into occupied units.

If access to a dwelling unit is necessary for inspection or repair coordination, follow the lease and applicable Florida access rules, including Florida Statute 83.53.

Record:

- why entry is needed
- units affected
- notice provided
- vendor identity
- property representative attending
- emergency basis, if applicable

## Equipment and parking

Map the route for:

- bucket truck
- lift
- crane
- loader
- stump grinder
- chipper
- log truck
- debris trailer
- mats
- crew vehicles

Check:

- gate width
- carport height
- speed bumps
- curb turns
- parked vehicles
- soft turf
- irrigation
- private lighting
- underground utilities
- fire lane
- trash pickup
- school or shuttle routes

Use [Can Heavy Tree Equipment Leave Ruts in a Florida Yard?](/blog/can-heavy-tree-equipment-leave-ruts-in-a-florida-yard/) for property-protection planning.

## Permit and ownership review

Before planned removal, confirm:

- parcel ownership
- master association control
- easements
- required landscape plan
- city or county permit
- protected-tree rules
- replacement planting
- stormwater or preserve restrictions
- right-of-way involvement

Florida Statute 163.045 uses a qualifying single-family detached definition. Do not assume an apartment or multifamily property receives the same treatment.

## Contractor documentation

Request:

- written estimate
- general liability insurance
- applicable workers’ compensation documentation
- subcontractor information
- business name matching documents
- equipment plan
- work-zone plan
- utility coordination
- cleanup and stump scope
- change-order procedure
- day-of supervisor

Coverage requirements can vary by business and work. Verify actual documentation rather than using a generic statement.

## Written scope

| Scope item | Multifamily-specific detail |
|---|---|
| Tree and method | Exact tree, pruning/removal, rigging/equipment |
| Resident control | Buildings, parking, sidewalks, amenities |
| Property protection | Roofs, balconies, carports, vehicles, curbs, pools |
| Utilities | Overhead, underground, irrigation, lighting |
| Debris | Staging, hauling time, blocked-space duration |
| Stump | Grinding, private systems, trip control, restoration |
| Emergency phase | What becomes safe today |
| Return phase | Remaining removal, hauling, stump, landscape |
| Reopening | Person who approves removal of barriers |

## Debris and stump safety

Debris should not:

- block a fire lane
- force residents into vehicle traffic
- cover irrigation controls
- obstruct mail or package access
- create sharp or unstable piles
- remain beside playgrounds or dog areas
- conceal an open stump hole

If a stump remains temporarily, barricade it and define the return date.

For permanent stump work, visit [stump grinding services](/services/stump-grinding/).

## Reopening checklist

Before reopening an affected area, confirm:

- no hanging or suspended wood remains
- access is clear
- sidewalks are free of debris
- vehicles can enter safely
- fire lane is open
- amenity surfaces are inspected
- stump area is safe
- irrigation or lighting damage is identified
- barriers are removed by an authorized person
- after-work photos are saved
