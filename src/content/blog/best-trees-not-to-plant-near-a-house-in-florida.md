# Best Trees Not to Plant Near a House in Florida

A lot of homeowners ask what the best tree is to plant near the house.

That is a good question. But in Florida, the better question often comes first: **Which trees should not be planted there at all?**

A tree that looks great in the nursery can turn into a long-term problem if it ends up too close to the house, driveway, pool deck, roofline, septic area, or utility path. The wrong species in the wrong place does not always fail quickly. More often, it creates a slow and expensive problem over years.

## “Bad near a house” is not only about roots

Roots matter, but they are not the whole story.

Trees can be poor choices near a house because of:

- mature canopy size,
- wide-spreading roots,
- storm failure potential,
- brittle wood,
- heavy fruit or limb drop,
- repeated pruning needs,
- gutter and roof contact,
- sidewalk, driveway, or pool-deck conflict,
- limited access for future removal.

The wrong tree near a house can create risk above ground and below ground at the same time.

## What to avoid near structures

| Tree behavior | Why it creates problems |
|---|---|
| Very large mature spread | Can overtake rooflines and small yards. |
| Heavy limb drop | Raises roof, car, and walkway risk. |
| Aggressive surface roots | Can conflict with hardscape and shallow soil areas. |
| Constant pruning need | Usually signals poor site fit. |
| Messy fruit or seed drop | Creates cleanup and slip concerns. |
| Weak storm performance | Increases risk near targets. |

If a tree was removed because it was too close to the house, do not repeat the same mistake with a replacement tree.
