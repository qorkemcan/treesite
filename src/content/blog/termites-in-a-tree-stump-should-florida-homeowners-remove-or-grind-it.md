# Termites in a Tree Stump: Should Florida Homeowners Remove or Grind It?

Termites in a stump confirm that termites are using dead wood in the yard. They do not, by themselves, prove that the house is infested.

Two professionals may have different roles:

- a licensed pest professional identifies the termite and evaluates structural risk
- a stump-grinding provider removes or reduces the dead-wood obstacle

When the stump is close to a house, deck, shed, fence, or other wood structure, coordinate the sequence before grinding.

## Use this stump-and-termite table

| Situation | Practical next step |
|---|---|
| Old stump far from structures, no other concern | Decide based on mowing, trip, appearance, or replanting needs |
| Active termites in stump near house or deck | Pest inspection before or alongside stump planning |
| Termites or swarmers also appear in the building | Contact a licensed pest professional promptly |
| Stump blocks landscaping or creates a trip hazard | Request a result-based grinding quote |
| Utility or private line may cross the root zone | Resolve locating before grinding |
| Stump is part of a living tree’s root system | Do not grind roots without evaluating the living tree |
| Fallen tree reveals hollow or termite-damaged wood | Photograph and inspect the surrounding property |

## Did termites kill the tree?

Often, termites arrive after wood has already died or decayed.

The original tree problem may have involved:

- storm injury
- root disease
- fungal decay
- flooding
- drought
- lightning
- construction
- trunk wounds
- advanced decline

Termite identification alone may not explain why the tree failed.

## Stump grinding versus full excavation

### Stump grinding

Grinding:

- reduces the central stump below the agreed grade
- leaves many roots underground
- produces wood-and-soil grindings
- is the usual residential option
- may be limited by utilities, hardscape, or structures

### Full excavation

Excavation:

- removes more stump and root material
- disturbs a wider area
- may require heavier equipment
- is usually linked to construction or major site preparation
- can create additional utility and restoration concerns

Termite activity does not automatically require full root excavation.

## Sequence near a structure

Before disturbing a termite-active stump close to a building:

1. photograph the activity
2. note the distance to the structure
3. look for mud tubes, damaged wood, wings, or swarmers
4. ask a licensed pest professional whether inspection should occur before grinding
5. decide how grindings will be handled
6. create the required Sunshine 811 ticket
7. identify private irrigation, pool, lighting, septic, and owner-maintained lines
8. define the final stump result

Grinding the stump does not provide structural termite treatment.

## Chips and grindings

Ask whether grindings will be:

- left in the hole
- piled onsite
- spread in a bed
- partially removed
- fully hauled
- replaced with clean soil

When termites are active near a structure, avoid piling wood-rich grindings against:

- foundation
- siding
- deck
- fence
- shed
- garage
- crawlspace

The pest professional and tree provider can help coordinate the handling plan, but the written quote should assign responsibility.

## Call 811 before grinding

Sunshine 811 advises homeowners to notify 811 before grinding or pulling a stump.

The ticket coordinates participating underground-facility operators. It may not locate private systems such as:

- irrigation
- pool plumbing
- landscape lighting
- private electric
- customer-owned water or sewer
- drainage
- pet fence

Use [Florida 811 Before Planting or Stump Grinding](/blog/do-you-need-to-call-811-before-tree-planting-or-stump-grinding-in-florida/) for the full workflow.

## When stump grinding makes sense

Grinding may be useful when the stump:

- provides dead wood close to a structure
- interferes with mowing
- creates a trip hazard
- blocks sod or planting
- is decaying
- sits near a driveway or walkway
- prevents fence or landscape work
- has become a recurring pest or moisture concern

The intended use should determine the depth and cleanup scope.

Use [How Deep Should a Stump Be Ground in a Florida Yard?](/blog/how-deep-should-a-stump-be-ground-in-a-florida-yard/) for result-based wording.

## When a second visit may be needed

A return may be needed if:

- utility locating limits the first visit
- hidden metal or concrete appears
- private lines are discovered
- a fence panel must be removed later
- chips settle and expose included wood
- the homeowner changes the end use

Use [Why a Stump Sometimes Needs a Second Grinding Visit](/blog/why-a-stump-sometimes-needs-a-second-grinding-visit/) to distinguish callback from added work.

## What not to do

Do not:

- spray random chemicals into the stump
- burn the stump
- assume grinding treats the house
- assume the house is infested without inspection
- bury termite-active wood beside the foundation
- grind before utility and private-line questions are resolved
- remove roots belonging to a living tree without assessment

## What to photograph

Send:

1. termites or swarmers
2. stump interior
3. mud tubes or pellets
4. distance to house or deck
5. moisture and irrigation
6. entire stump and root flare
7. access route
8. pavers or structures
9. intended finished use
