# 24/7 Emergency Tree Service: What Defines a Tree Emergency?

A tree emergency is an active threat that should not be left in place until ordinary scheduling.

The clearest examples are a tree or limb touching power lines, a tree moving toward an occupied structure, a split trunk opening under load, large wood suspended over an entry or driveway, a tree resting on a roof, or a failure blocking the only safe access to the property.

A yard covered with stable branches can look dramatic without being a 24/7 emergency. A single cracked limb above a bedroom can look less dramatic and be far more urgent.

Judge the situation by what can still move, what it can hit, and whether people can stay safely away.

## Use this three-level triage

### Level 1: call 911 or the utility first

Tree-service dispatch is not the first call when there is:

- a downed electrical line,
- a branch or tree touching electrical equipment,
- fire, sparking, arcing, or smoke,
- a person injured or trapped,
- a tree preventing emergency responders from reaching someone,
- an immediate collapse threat to an occupied area.

Stay away from the tree and anything it touches. Electricity can travel through wood, fences, wet ground, vehicles, and other conductive paths.

### Level 2: request emergency tree service now

[Emergency response services](/services/emergency-response/) are reasonable when the electrical and life-safety channels have been addressed but an active tree hazard remains, such as:

- suspended limb over an entry,
- tree resting on a roof,
- trunk splitting under load,
- newly leaning tree toward a target,
- hung-up tree caught in another tree,
- driveway blocked by heavy wood,
- large branch over a bedroom, walkway, or vehicle.

### Level 3: schedule normal tree service

Ordinary scheduling may be enough when debris is fully down, stable, away from targets, and easy to isolate until daylight.

That may still require [tree removal services](/services/tree-removal/) or [tree trimming services](/services/tree-trimming/), but not necessarily 24/7 dispatch.

## What does not automatically define an emergency?

Not every storm mess is an emergency.

These may be urgent, but not always 24/7 emergency situations:

| Situation | Why it may wait |
|---|---|
| Small branches down | Stable debris may be isolated. |
| Tree down in unused area | No active target may be present. |
| Stump remains | Stump work is usually later. |
| Leaves and palm fronds everywhere | Cleanup may not be hazardous. |
| Old lean with no change | Monitoring or evaluation may be enough. |

[Stump grinding services](/services/stump-grinding/) and final cleanup often happen after the immediate risk is controlled.

## What to tell the dispatcher

Be ready to explain:

- whether lines are involved,
- what the tree is touching,
- whether anything is still moving,
- whether access is blocked,
- whether a roof or structure is affected,
- whether people can stay out of the area,
- whether photos are available.
