# Best Trees to Plant Under Power Lines in Florida

Planting under power lines is one of the easiest ways to create a future tree problem on purpose.

A lot of homeowners plant whatever looks good in the strip of yard they have available, then act surprised years later when the tree has to be cut back hard, shaped awkwardly, or removed because it outgrew the space directly beneath the wires.

In Florida, the best trees for these spaces are usually the ones that stay small at maturity, have a form that fits narrow overhead space, and do not force the utility or homeowner into constant corrective pruning.

## Plant for mature height, not nursery size

A tree in a container may look harmless beneath overhead lines. That is not what matters.

The question that counts is: **How tall and wide will this tree be when mature?**

Under or near overhead distribution lines, the safest choice is a small-maturing tree, not a tree the homeowner hopes to keep small later through trimming.

## What to check before planting

| Question | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| How tall will it mature? | Prevents future conflict with wires. |
| How wide will it spread? | Clearance is horizontal as well as vertical. |
| Is there underground utility risk? | Call before digging. |
| Who controls pruning near the line? | Utilities may control electrical clearance. |
| Is the space too narrow? | Some strips are better without trees. |
| Is the tree storm-resilient for the site? | Florida wind exposure matters. |

Never prune near energized lines yourself. Contact the utility when branches are close to electrical equipment.
