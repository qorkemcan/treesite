# Charlotte County Tree Removal Guide: Residential Rules, Costs, and Common Job Types

If you own residential property in Charlotte County and need a tree removed, the first question usually is not cost.

It is this: **Do I need approval first, or can this tree come down now?**

That question matters because tree removal in Charlotte County is not just a chainsaw decision. Depending on the tree, its size, its location, and whether it is truly hazardous, the job can involve county rules, Florida state law, and practical site conditions at the same time.

The smartest homeowners treat tree removal as a documentation and planning problem first, and a cutting problem second.

## Ordinary removal and hazardous-tree removal are different paths

A local permit requirement and Florida’s residential hazardous-tree law are not automatically contradictory. They may apply in different situations.

The better question is not only: **Is there a permit rule?**

It is also: **Is this an ordinary removal, or is this a documented hazardous-tree situation?**

That distinction affects timing, documentation, and how confidently a homeowner can move forward.

## What Charlotte County homeowners should verify

| Question | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Is the property inside unincorporated Charlotte County or a municipality? | Local jurisdiction changes the process. |
| Is the tree above the county threshold? | Size can trigger permit review. |
| Is the tree hazardous and properly documented? | Florida law may affect local requirements. |
| Is the tree near structures, roads, or utilities? | Site risk changes urgency and method. |
| Is stump grinding included? | Removal approval does not define final cleanup. |
| Is replanting or mitigation involved? | Some removals may have follow-up obligations. |

Verify the current county requirement directly before work begins.
