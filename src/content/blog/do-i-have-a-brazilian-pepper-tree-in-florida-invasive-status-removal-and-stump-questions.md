# Do I Have a Brazilian Pepper Tree in Florida? Invasive Status, Removal, and Stump Questions

Brazilian pepper is a prohibited nonnative plant in Florida, but a legal plant-status label does not by itself answer who owns the tree, whether local approval is needed, or how a specific specimen should be removed.

Start with three separate decisions:

1. Confirm that the plant is Brazilian pepper.
2. Control exposure, seeds, sprouts, and sensitive-site risks.
3. Build a written removal and follow-up scope.

Brazilian pepper is in the cashew family, the same family as poison ivy. Florida agencies warn that sap and plant material can irritate skin and the respiratory system in sensitive people. Do not burn it.

## Identification should use several features

Brazilian pepper commonly grows as a dense shrub or multi-stemmed tree.

| Feature | Brazilian pepper pattern |
|---|---|
| Leaves | Alternate, odd-pinnately compound leaves with several leaflets. |
| Leaf rachis | May be reddish or slightly winged. |
| Fruit | Clusters of small red berries on female plants. |
| Habit | Dense branching, thickets, volunteer seedlings. |
| Site | Fence lines, disturbed edges, canals, wetlands, vacant lots. |

Do not identify Brazilian pepper from red berries alone. Photograph the full plant, both sides of a complete leaf, fruit when present, bark, stems, and the surrounding site. Use [Florida Tree Identification for Homeowners](/blog/florida-tree-identification-for-homeowners-common-yard-trees-and-what-they-can-tell-you/) when uncertain.

## Removal planning is more than cutting

| Removal question | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Is the site near wetlands, canals, or conservation areas? | Environmental authority may apply. |
| Are seeds or berries present? | Debris containment and cleanup matter. |
| Is stump resprouting likely? | Cut-stump follow-up may be needed. |
| Are utilities or private systems nearby? | Call 811 and check irrigation or septic. |
| Is the site large or overgrown? | Clearing scope may be different from one-tree removal. |
