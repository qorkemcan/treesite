## Main findings

### Legal and compliance scope

- The five legal/compliance articles use generally sensible caution but often convert broad legal concepts into conclusions without current statutes, ordinances, governing documents, notices, deadlines, or case-specific facts.
- None should predict liability, HOA authority, fines, permit outcomes, or work authority.
- Each needs a clear boundary between physical tree work and legal, insurance, code-enforcement, association, survey, utility, or regulatory decisions.

### Jurisdiction and evidence

- Street-edge work needs the exact controlling owner or agency, location evidence, written authorization, utility and drainage review, and a local permit-office workflow.
- HOA work needs governing documents, parcel/common-area status, architectural procedures, emergency notice, local permits, and written approval.
- After-the-fact cases need the actual notice, issuing authority, response deadline, evidence file, and hearing or appeal route.

### Liability response

- The falling-tree page must shift from general negligence explanation to a safety-insurer-evidence-attorney workflow.
- ProTreeTrim should never assign fault or imply a remote tree conversation predicts legal liability.

### Disease handling

- Disease removal is the only primarily technical article in this package, but its recommendations must be organism-specific.
- Diagnosis, tool sanitation, vectors, root connections, wood movement, chips, firewood, stump treatment, and disposal should not be generalized across diseases.

### Publishing quality

- All six need H1 headings, official sources, current dates, decision tables, internal links, service boundaries, and more varied prose structures.
- Repetitive question-answer-mistake-guidance-takeaway patterns remain visible.

## Recommended final revision order

1. Falling Tree Liability — highest legal and claims risk.
2. HOA Approval — broad and potentially misleading legal conclusions.
3. Improper Pruning Fines — needs official ordinance workflow and less absolute framing.
4. After-the-Fact Permits — needs notice, deadline, issuing-office, and legal-response process.
5. Street, Sidewalk, or Right-of-Way — strong base; add location and authority workflow.
6. Diseased Tree Removal — build organism-specific diagnostic and handling matrix.
