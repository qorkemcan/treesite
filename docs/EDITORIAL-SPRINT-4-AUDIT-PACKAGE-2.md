## Main findings

### Diagnosis before treatment

- Deep-root fertilization is the weakest article in this group because the headline promises a health boost before confirming nutrient need, soil conditions, species requirements, irrigation, or a suitable application method.
- Air excavation, root pruning, soil treatment, utility exposure, and root-collar work must be separated rather than sold as one combined solution.

### Specialized preservation systems

- Lightning protection needs a candidacy, design, installation, grounding, inspection, maintenance, and post-strike workflow.
- The dispatch CTA must not imply that a remote conversation can determine system design or whether a tree is structurally worth protecting.

### Construction and property protection

- Paver and artificial-turf risk depends on excavation, base preparation, compaction, grade, permeability, heat, open soil, species, tree condition, and project footprint—not on one surface label alone.
- Driveway, septic, irrigation, lighting, pool, and private-utility protection require a mapped site plan, pre-existing-condition photos, equipment routes, no-go zones, and written responsibility.

### Storm and lightning risk

- Flooded-soil content should distinguish oxygen stress, softened soil resistance, erosion, prior defects, and actual root-plate movement.
- Lightning-struck-tree content needs electrical and fire screening before save-versus-remove discussion.
- High-wind failure content should teach failure patterns and actions without pretending to predict exactly how a tree will fail.

### Publishing quality

- All eight lack the established H1, decision-table, source, internal-link, current-date, and service-boundary package.
- Repeated Short Answer, warning lists, mistakes, questions, professional guidance, and final takeaway structures create a visible AI pattern.

## Recommended revision order

1. Deep Root Fertilization — diagnosis and treatment claims.

4. Tree Removal Near Driveway, Septic, and Irrigation — property-protection scope.
