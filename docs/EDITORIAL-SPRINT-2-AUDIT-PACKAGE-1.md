## Common findings

### Search-intent gaps

- Three price-led articles do not provide a durable, sourced pricing framework.
- General cost, large-tree cost, oak cost, dead-tree cost, estimate, and quote-comparison pages repeat the same access/target/cleanup explanation without sharply separated roles.
- The quote-comparison article overlaps with several Sprint 1 articles and should become a focused comparison tool rather than another exhaustive hiring guide.

### AI-pattern signals

- Repeated `Short Answer` openings.
- Predictable list-heavy structure.
- Recurrent phrases such as “the real question,” “not just height,” and “final takeaway.”
- Similar CTA language and repeated warnings that the lowest quote may not be the best.

### Internal-link gaps

- None of the eight articles directly links to `/services/tree-removal/` or another relevant service route.
- The strongest commercial pages remain isolated from the money pages they should support.
- The quote-comparison article contains a visible editorial note instead of integrated links and references a retired cheapest-quote slug.

### CTA gaps

- Three articles have no meaningful CTA.
- Phone-only CTAs do not clarify whether the next action is planned removal, emergency response, stump grinding, or an estimate.
- CTAs should ask for tree photos, target information, gate/access measurements, and stump/cleanup expectations.

### Accuracy and trust issues

- The large-tree cost article contains visible citation markers.
- Price ranges need dates, methodology, and careful local-market caveats.
- The yard-protection article should not imply that equipment traffic over septic areas becomes acceptable simply because mats are used.
- Power-line references should prioritize utility coordination and qualified line-clearance work.
