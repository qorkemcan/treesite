## Current-source checkpoints

### Arborist versus general tree crew

Helpful distinction page, but it needs a clear H1, source normalization, service routes, and a stronger credential/scope boundary. The revision should explain when routine work is enough and when evaluation, documentation, risk assessment, or preservation judgment changes the decision.

### Rural Masaryktown arborist decisions

Useful rural-property page with good context around drives, barns, gates, and land use. Needs H1, source section, direct service routes, stronger Hernando/Pasco-area local verification caution, and clearer limits around arborist credentials and remote diagnosis.

### Florida Keys tree service

Strong local theme around salt, wind, narrow access, and coastal exposure. Needs H1, official-source cleanup, service routing, and careful boundaries around Keys permitting, mangroves, utilities, and storm-readiness claims.

### Jacksonville winter maintenance

Good North Florida seasonal angle, but it reads like an older standalone guide. Needs H1, source section, trimming/risk-assessment routes, freeze-injury caution, and a more decision-oriented structure.

### The Villages and senior communities

Strong topic because tree care in senior/community settings involves access, trip hazards, low-maintenance priorities, HOA/community documents, and storm prep. Needs H1, source section, service routes, and non-stereotyping wording.

### Salt spray damage

Useful coastal tree-health guide. Needs H1, source section, internal links, and a stronger non-diagnostic boundary so the article does not imply salt injury can be confirmed remotely or repaired with one universal treatment.

### Selling a home and tree value

High-value homeowner decision topic. Needs H1, source section, service routes, pre-sale documentation links, and careful real-estate/appraisal/legal boundaries.

### Hurricane-resistant trees

Very high search value but needs careful rewording. No tree should be framed as hurricane-proof. The revision should preserve planting usefulness while adding source support, maintenance/placement context, and storm-resilience caveats.

### Wood chips after stump grinding

Practical post-service page. Needs H1, source section, stump grinding/replanting routes, internal links, and clearer limits around mulch depth, termites, nitrogen concerns, and where not to use chips.

### Adding soil around trees

Strong educational content about root flare and grade change. Needs H1, source normalization, internal links, and site-specific cautions around buried flare, drainage, exposed roots, construction fill, and professional evaluation.
