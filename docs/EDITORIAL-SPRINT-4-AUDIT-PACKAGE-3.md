## Main findings

### Post-storm sequence

- The inspection article has the correct safety premise but must screen utilities, fire, floodwater, structure loading, and active movement before a homeowner walk-around.
- The debris article needs to distinguish ordinary yard waste, loaded tree debris, utility responsibility, municipal pickup, building debris, hazardous contamination, hauling, and emergency tree work.

### Palm cluster overlap

- The three palm-pruning articles currently repeat the same selective-versus-aggressive-pruning message.
- Each page needs a distinct role:
  - hurricane-season page = pre-storm condition and whole-palm risk
  - bad-pruning page = harmful pruning patterns, written exclusions, and long-term correction
  - best-time page = calendar and condition timing hub
- Cold-snap recovery should own the wait-versus-prune timeline and spear/crown escalation triggers.

### Plant-health boundaries

- Oak pest content needs identification, oak-species context, season, tissue affected, infestation extent, beneficial insects, treatment thresholds, and label/licensing boundaries.
- Citrus greening has good source-based content but exposes citation-tool artifacts on the public page. This is the most urgent publishing defect in the package.
- Plant-health diagnosis and treatment should not be routed automatically to tree removal or generic dispatch.

### Publishing quality

- All eight pages need the established H1, decision table, source section, contextual internal links, current date, and narrow service boundaries.
- Repeated Short Answer, common mistake, better questions, practical mindset, and final takeaway structures create visible AI-pattern risk.

## Recommended revision order

1. Citrus Greening — remove public citation artifacts and rebuild sources/decision flow.
2. Debris Cleanup — clarify responsibility, hazards, hauling, and municipal/private boundaries.
3. Best Time to Trim Palms — give the overlapping cluster a timing-hub role.
4. Bad Palm Pruning — add standards, exclusions, and correction path.
5. Do Palms Need Trimming Before Hurricane Season — whole-palm and storm-risk matrix.
6. Cold Snap Recovery — spear/crown timeline and treatment boundaries.
7. Common Oak Pests — identification and threshold matrix.
8. Caring for Trees After a Storm — strongest starting structure; add exclusions, links, and routes.
