## Main findings

### Legacy emergency and hurricane hubs

- Both pages have useful safety and defect content but lack the emergency-service hierarchy now established elsewhere on the site.
- They need 911, electric utility, fire/gas, evacuation, exclusion-zone, insurer, staged make-safe, and service-phase logic.
- Hurricane language must avoid predicting whether a tree will survive a future storm.

### Risk and pruning decisions

- The falling-tree page is a strong triage draft but needs formal risk boundaries and a red/amber/green structure.
- The pruning-versus-removal page needs preservation alternatives, mitigation effectiveness, and authorization layers.
- The cut/no-cut page should become an annotated pruning anatomy and stop-work guide rather than a universal list of tree parts that should never be cut.

### Neighbor incident

- The neighbor-property page is legally sensitive and currently lacks source, insurer, cleanup-authority, property-entry, and attorney boundaries.
- Coverage and legal liability must remain separate.

### Mature-tree cost and relocation

- The 100-year-old-tree page has strong commercial value but needs dated methodology and should avoid unsupported statewide price ranges.
- The relocation page is the strongest current article, but it must clarify specialist scope, preparation, warranty, establishment, and ProTreeTrim’s service boundary.

## Recommended revision order
