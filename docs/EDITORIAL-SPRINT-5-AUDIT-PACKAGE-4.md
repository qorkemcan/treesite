## Main findings

### Coastal edges and erosion

- Waterfront and wall-adjacent pages correctly recognize that tree risk can be a site-system problem rather than a tree-only problem.
- They need clear separation among tree assessment, drainage, slope stabilization, geotechnical, structural, marine, seawall, canal-bank, and public-authority roles.
- Tree-service CTAs should not imply diagnosis of wall, bank, or drainage systems.

### Wildfire

- The defensible-space page has high potential but visible public citation artifacts must be removed.
- The article needs a home-ignition-zone framework, ember and structure pathways, local fire/forestry authority involvement, and site-specific guidance rather than broad universal spacing claims.

### Storm and lightning scope

- Storm cleanup versus removal is the strongest current article and should become a staged conversion page.
- Lightning content needs a four-way split: active storm or electrical/fire emergency, immediate structural hazard, prompt assessment, and documented monitoring.
- Neither article currently includes sufficient utility, insurer, permit, site-reopening, or service-phase detail.

### Clearing and removal

- The clearing page owns a valuable commercial intent but needs sharper definitions and an end-use-first scope.
- It must distinguish removal, selective clearing, brush clearing, broad clearing, grubbing, stump work, debris strategy, preservation, utilities, drainage, wetlands, wildlife, and finish level.

### Publishing quality

- All six require H1 headings, current update dates, decision tables, contextual links, direct service or authority routes, official sources, and specialist boundaries.
- Repetitive explanatory patterns remain visible, especially repeated 'why' sections and final-takeaway restatements.

## Recommended revision order
