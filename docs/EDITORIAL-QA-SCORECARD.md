# Editorial QA Scorecard

Use this 100-point scorecard to evaluate article quality consistently across weekly editorial QA reviews.

## 100-Point Scoring Model

| Category | Points | Scoring Guidance |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Headline | 10 | Clear, natural, search-aligned, and not keyword-stuffed. |
| Intro | 10 | Fast, useful, homeowner-focused, and free of filler. |
| Readability | 10 | Easy to scan, logical, direct, and well paced. |
| US English | 10 | Natural American English with clean grammar and phrasing. |
| AI detection risk | 10 | Low risk of sounding generic, repetitive, or machine-written. |
| Florida relevance | 10 | Includes useful Florida context where appropriate. |
| Decision support | 10 | Helps the reader compare options, risks, and next steps. |
| Internal links | 10 | Uses relevant, natural links that support the reader journey. |
| CTA | 10 | Clear, calm, useful, and aligned with the article's intent. |
| Overall polish | 10 | Consistent, trustworthy, accurate, and ready for publication. |

## Score Bands

| Score | Result | Meaning |
| ---: | --- | --- |
| 95-100 | Excellent | Ready to publish or maintain with routine updates. |
| 90-94 | Minor polish | Strong article with a small number of editorial refinements needed. |
| 80-89 | Needs revision | Useful article, but several quality issues should be corrected. |
| Below 80 | Major rewrite | Article needs substantial revision before it meets quality standards. |

## Scoring Notes

- Score the article as it exists today, not based on its ranking potential.
- Be specific when subtracting points.
- Do not use the scorecard to reward keyword density.
- Do not penalize an article for being concise if it fully satisfies the reader's need.
- When in doubt, prioritize usefulness, trust, and natural language.
