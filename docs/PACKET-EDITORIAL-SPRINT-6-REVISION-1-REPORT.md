## High-stakes corrections

### Storm-damaged tree

- Introduced the correct life-safety and utility hierarchy.
- Added exclusion-zone, documentation, insurer, staged make-safe, standing-tree assessment, cleanup, and reopening logic.
- Separated electrical hazards from private tree work.

### Hurricane risk

- Removed the implication that an article or inspection can predict whether a tree will survive a future hurricane.
- Reframed the page around defects, targets, practical mitigation, monitoring, and timing.
- Added a clear warning against last-minute storm panic pruning.

### Likely-to-fall guide

- Added red, amber, and monitored-condition lanes.
- Distinguished homeowner observation from a qualified tree-risk assessment.
- Added target, time-frame, and assessment-scope questions.

### Removal versus pruning

- Added preservation alternatives: structural pruning, support, target management, root-zone care, and monitoring.
- Replaced the overly absolute “branch problem versus tree problem” slogan with a mitigation-effectiveness framework.
- Added permit, HOA, utility, wildlife, and written-scope checks.

### Cut/no-cut guide

- Reframed universal “never cut” language into accepted cut-location and stop-work guidance.
- Added branch collar, leader, root, root flare, palm bud, major limb, tension-loaded wood, and utility distinctions.
- Added a practical homeowner stop line.

### Neighbor-property incident

- Separated safety, insurance coverage, legal liability, access, evidence, and cleanup authority.
- Added current context that the proposed 2025 Fallen Tree Act did not become law.
- Added insurer and attorney escalation criteria without declaring fault.

### Mature-tree cost

- Removed unsupported statewide price ranges.
- Added quote-driver, access, method, end-use, permit, preservation, and line-item comparison frameworks.
- Clarified that age alone does not determine price.

### Mature-tree relocation

- Clarified specialist scope and that ordinary removal providers may not perform relocation.
- Added destination design, root preparation, access, move-day planning, aftercare, warranty, permit, utility, and professional-role sections.
- Separated retention, relocation, and removal/replanting.
