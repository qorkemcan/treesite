## Package logic

### Package 1 — Pricing, quotes, and high-intent removal
The first eight pages sit closest to estimate requests, tree-removal calls, and quote comparison. They should also be checked together for overlap among general cost, large-tree cost, oak cost, dead-tree cost, estimate, and quote-comparison intent.

### Package 2 — Access, equipment, property protection, and stump scope
These eight pages explain why a job becomes complex or expensive. They can support tree-removal and stump-grinding money pages while reducing homeowner uncertainty before a call.

### Package 3 — Risk diagnosis that can lead to removal or emergency service
These pages address visible defects with high homeowner anxiety. The audit should focus on technical accuracy, remote-diagnosis limits, urgent-versus-scheduled action, and natural service-page links.

### Package 4 — Local commercial support and stump completion
The final six pages connect selected local money pages and stump-grinding decisions to practical Florida access and finish expectations.
