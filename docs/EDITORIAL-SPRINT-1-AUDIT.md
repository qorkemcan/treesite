## Common AI-Pattern Issues

- Repeated `Short Answer` structure across many posts makes the library feel templated.
- Several articles end with generic `Final Takeaway` sections.
- Many sections use predictable patterns such as `Better Questions to Ask` and `Common Homeowner Mistakes`.
- Some articles overuse exhaustive list structures without enough concrete Florida examples.
- Legal articles include visible citation artifacts such as `turn...search` references that must be removed before promotion.
- Some older emergency articles lack the immediate homeowner-first answer newer posts provide.

## Common Internal Link Gaps

- Many high-intent articles have zero internal links.
- Several hiring articles link to stale or likely missing target URLs.
- Some links use absolute `https://www.protreetrim.com/...` URLs where site-relative internal links would be cleaner.
- Emergency articles often do not link to insurance, photo documentation, driveway-blocked, or storm cleanup support articles.
- Permit and Florida Statute articles do not link into county hubs, Orange County guide content, arborist-report content, or hazardous-tree decision content.
- Roots and palm articles need stronger links to risk assessment, tree removal decision, and service-area next steps.

## Common CTA Issues

- Many CTAs are missing entirely.
- Some CTAs are buried in body copy rather than framed as a clear next step.
- Emergency articles need clearer urgency-specific CTAs: call utility / emergency services first when applicable, then request local tree help.
- Legal articles need careful CTAs that encourage verification and local review without overclaiming.
- Hiring articles should lead readers toward comparing quotes, requesting local assistance, or reading related estimate/red-flag resources.
