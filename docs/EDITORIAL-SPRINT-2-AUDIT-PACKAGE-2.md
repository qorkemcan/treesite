## Common findings

### Search-intent overlap

- The three crane/roof articles repeat the same crane-versus-rigging, access, paver, septic, and power-line explanations.
- The two property-protection articles repeat mats, pavers, wet lawns, irrigation, and homeowner-preparation sections.
- The three stump articles repeat gate width, utilities, chips, depth, surface roots, pavers, and replanting.

Each page needs one clear role rather than a slightly different version of the same checklist.

### Accuracy and trust

- `how-deep-should-a-stump-be-ground...` presents 6–12 inches as a common standard without a cited industry or equipment basis.
- Crane setup language should make clear that ground bearing, outrigger support, capacity, and lift planning require qualified site evaluation.
- Septic tanks and drain fields should be treated as no-drive constraints, not ordinary surfaces made safe by mats.
- Public utility marking and private irrigation/pool/lighting systems need separate explanations.
- Power-line sections should prioritize utility coordination and qualified line-clearance work.

### Internal links and CTAs

- None of the eight pages links directly to `/services/tree-removal/` or `/services/stump-grinding/`.
- `what-makes-stump-grinding-harder-than-it-looks.md` links to the wrong shortened gate-access slug.
- Phone-only CTAs do not route the homeowner to the correct service or specify which photos and measurements to provide.

### AI-pattern signals

- Repeated `Short Answer` openings.
- Similar sequences of explanation → homeowner mistakes → questions → FAQ → final takeaway.
- Heavy list density and repeated phrases such as “the real question,” “not magic,” and “the plan matters.”
