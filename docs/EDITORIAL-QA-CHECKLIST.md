# Editorial QA Checklist

Use this checklist during each article review. Mark each item as pass, polish, or revise.

## Headline

- Clearly reflects the article topic.
- Matches the likely search intent.
- Sounds natural to a US homeowner.
- Avoids forced keywords, hype, and awkward phrasing.

## Intro

- Gets to the point quickly.
- Confirms the reader is in the right place.
- Sets up the practical value of the article.
- Avoids generic filler, broad claims, and slow background paragraphs.

## Search Intent

- Answers the likely reason the reader searched.
- Stays focused on the promised topic.
- Does not drift into unrelated service or SEO filler.
- Covers the decision points a homeowner would expect.

## Short Answer

- Provides a clear early answer where appropriate.
- Gives the reader immediate value before deeper explanation.
- Avoids overpromising or oversimplifying.
- Uses plain language.

## Decision Support

- Helps the homeowner understand options, risks, tradeoffs, and next steps.
- Explains when professional help is appropriate.
- Includes practical factors such as cost, timing, severity, safety, permits, or urgency when relevant.
- Avoids vague advice that does not help the reader act.

## Natural American English

- Uses natural US spelling, phrasing, and sentence rhythm.
- Avoids stiff, translated, or overly formal wording.
- Uses contractions only where they fit the site's tone.
- Keeps sentences clear and readable.

## AI-Pattern Review

- Removes repetitive sentence structures.
- Flags generic transitions such as "In conclusion" or "It is important to note" when they feel mechanical.
- Avoids vague summaries that repeat earlier sections without adding value.
- Looks for phrases that sound polished but empty.

## Terminology Review

- Uses tree care terms accurately.
- Avoids claiming arborist diagnosis unless the page clearly supports it.
- Distinguishes trimming, pruning, removal, stump grinding, storm cleanup, and emergency response.
- Avoids overstating ProTreeTrim's role as a direct tree-work contractor.

## Florida Context

- Adds Florida relevance where it helps the reader.
- Considers hurricanes, storm damage, palms, live oaks, mangroves, heat, humidity, pests, permits, and local service conditions.
- Avoids generic national advice when Florida-specific guidance would be more useful.
- Does not add Florida mentions just for keyword density.

## Internal Links

- Identifies relevant ProTreeTrim pages to link.
- Uses natural anchor text.
- Supports the reader's next step.
- Avoids repeated links to the same target unless clearly useful.

## CTA

- Gives the homeowner a clear next step.
- Matches the article's intent and urgency.
- Uses calm, trustworthy language.
- Avoids pressure, exaggerated urgency, or unsupported claims.

## Image Opportunity

- Notes whether the article would benefit from an image, diagram, service visual, storm example, species example, or homeowner decision aid.
- Identifies where the image should appear.
- Flags whether an image prompt is needed.
- Avoids decorative images that do not help the reader.

## Schema Review

- Confirms the article type and schema opportunity are appropriate.
- Flags FAQ, HowTo, Article, or local service schema opportunities only when the visible content supports them.
- Avoids schema that overstates services, credentials, or facts.
- Checks that schema recommendations align with current page content.

## Overall Consistency

- Aligns with ProTreeTrim's homeowner-first editorial standards.
- Maintains a consistent voice from start to finish.
- Avoids contradictions across sections.
- Supports trust, clarity, and practical decision-making.
