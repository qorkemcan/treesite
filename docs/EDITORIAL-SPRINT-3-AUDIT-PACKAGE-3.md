## Main findings

### Legal and insurance sensitivity

- The articles should organize practical decisions without deciding liability, coverage, habitability, association authority, or lease rights.
- Florida Statutes sections 83.51, 83.53, and 83.63 can be relevant to rental damage, but application depends on the tenancy, property, lease, code environment, tenant conduct, and facts.
- Section 718.113 supports association responsibility for common-element maintenance, except declaration-assigned limited-common-element duties; it does not resolve every tree alteration or authorization issue.
- Chapter 720 governs HOA operation and compliance with governing documents, but does not replace the declaration, plats, easements, local rules, and legal advice.
- Section 163.045 defines residential property narrowly as a qualifying single-family detached building and should not be presented as a universal path for commercial, multifamily, condo-common-area, or HOA-common-area work.
- Vacation-rental lodging classifications do not determine tree-work authorization, insurance coverage, platform relief, or reopening safety.

### Major overlap

- Rental removal and rental damage currently compete rather than complement each other.
- HOA and condo pages repeat most of the same ownership, approval, permit, contractor, cleanup, and stump sections.
- Commercial and multifamily pages repeat operations and contractor material; the multifamily page should own resident communication and occupied-building logistics.
- Vacation rental should own guest relocation, amenity closure, photo-ready restoration, and rebooking.

### Publishing failures

- Six June articles expose editorial notes and raw URL lists.
- `tree-damage-on-rental-properties-who-handles-what.md` exposes multiple `turn...` citation codes.
- Neighbor responsibility and rental damage have no H1 headings.
- The neighbor article has no sources; the rental-damage article has no usable source section.

### Conversion boundaries

- Tree services can stabilize, remove, trim, haul, grind, and document their work.
- Tree providers should not decide legal responsibility, lease rights, association authority, insurance coverage, platform refunds, or structural occupancy.
- Each page needs one distinct service path rather than a generic list of every ProTreeTrim service.

## Recommended revision order

1. Tree Damage on Rental Properties — visible citation code and highest legal risk.

3. Tree Removal for Rental Properties — separate authorization workflow from casualty law.

7. Vacation Rental — guest safety, amenities, restoration, reopening.
