## Current-source checkpoints

### Alternatives to cutting down a tree

The existing article has useful intent, but it needs to move from a generic alternatives list to a decision framework. It should separate:

- deadwood removal,
- structural pruning,
- clearance pruning,
- cabling or bracing,
- root-zone protection,
- target relocation,
- monitoring,
- hardscape adjustment,
- pest or plant-health diagnosis,
- removal when residual risk remains unacceptable.

The revision must avoid implying that every tree can be saved. It should explain that alternatives depend on the defect, target, species, site, owner tolerance, cost, and follow-up maintenance.

### Tree falls but misses the house

The existing article is useful but needs stronger storm-cleanup triage. It should separate:

- immediate safety,
- utilities,
- access,
- leaning or hung-up trees,
- fence or neighbor impact,
- root plate and remaining stump,
- documentation,
- insurance boundary,
- emergency versus scheduled cleanup,
- debris and stump scope.

The revision should avoid implying insurance coverage. It should say to document before cleanup and check the policy or carrier when coverage is unclear.

### Stump grinding near utilities

This is a high-consequence article and should become a strict pre-work checklist. It needs a stronger distinction between:

- public utility notification,
- private facilities,
- irrigation,
- pool lines,
- landscape lighting,
- gas grill lines,
- septic and drainage,
- grinding depth,
- lateral root grinding,
- stop-work triggers,
- hidden objects,
- written responsibility.

Sunshine 811 guidance should be used for public utility notification and private-facility limits. The article must not imply that public utility marking finds every buried line.

### Roots after tree removal

The existing roots-after-removal article needs to clarify what usually stops, what may remain, and what still matters. It should separate:

- most roots no longer actively growing once the tree is removed,
- species that may resprout,
- dead roots decaying over time,
- settlement,
- underground voids,
- hardscape and pipe concerns,
- replanting limits,
- stump grinding depth,
- root removal versus ordinary grinding,
- utility boundaries.

The revision should avoid absolute promises that roots are harmless after removal.

### Mushy tree beds

The mushy tree-bed article needs a diagnosis matrix, not a single wetness explanation. It should separate:

- irrigation overwatering,
- drainage obstruction,
- compacted soil,
- roof runoff,
- grade problems,
- decomposed mulch,
- mulch volcanoes,
- buried root flare,
- root-zone oxygen stress,
- fungal growth,
- mosquito or odor clues,
- when to call irrigation, drainage, or tree professionals.

The revision should avoid telling homeowners to dry the root zone completely.

### White stuff on mulch around Florida trees

This article already has a H1 and a stronger base than the others, but it still needs final Sprint 8 routing and decision polish. It should separate:

- normal fungal decomposition,
- slime mold,
- white mycelium in mulch,
- overmulching,
- buried root flare,
- white growth attached to trunk or roots,
- conks or structural fungi,
- pet and child exposure,
- tree decline symptoms,
- when not to spray fungicide.

The revision should preserve the good mold-versus-problem distinction but add direct service routes and stronger internal discovery.
