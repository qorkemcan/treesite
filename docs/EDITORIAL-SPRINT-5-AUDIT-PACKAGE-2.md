### Neighbor-content overlap

- The broad neighbor-dispute article should own the cluster-level routing decision.

- The branch-return article should own debris handling, local yard-waste rules, haul-away, and the do-not-dump answer.

### Property control and infrastructure

- HOA-root and sidewalk-root pages need records-first workflows rather than predictions about who pays.
- Sidewalk responsibility varies locally and requires the exact tree location, right-of-way, ordinance, notice, public authority, and repair authorization.
- Utility-easement content must distinguish land ownership, recorded easement rights, energized-line control, utility vegetation management, 811 locating, private facilities, and authorized private work.

### Environmental compliance

- The mangrove page carries the highest regulatory risk.
- Existing public citation artifacts must be removed.
- Every RMF, height, exemption, PMT, permit, defoliation, and delegated-program statement needs current official Florida DEP or statutory support.
- ProTreeTrim should not represent itself as deciding mangrove exemption or permit status.

### Publishing quality

- All eight need H1 headings, current update dates, decision tables, contextual links, direct service or authority routes, official sources, and explicit legal/compliance boundaries.
- Repetitive formulae remain visible: emotional setup, general rule, common mistake, better question, professional guidance, and final takeaway.
